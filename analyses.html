characters with heads: mi, sina, ona, lape

mi, sina and ona are the 1st, 2nd and 3rd person pronouns respectively, and lape means “sleep/rest”. All 4 are a smaller circle with a line appended. The pronouns's lines are tangent to the circle, while lape's juts out perpendicular to the circle.

mi's circle is above, the line tangent to its left side. sina's circle sits below, the line also tangent to its left side. ona's circle sits to the left, the line tangent to its bottom side. lape's circle also sits to the left, but the line comes off its leftmost side, horizontally. The line can be curved or straight.

the circle should not be more than 1/2 the em height, so they can't be confused for each other (see early font linja pona below).
