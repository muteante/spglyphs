This document should not be used as a prescriptive guide for how sitelen pona characters ought to look like. This document aims to provide a guide to those who want to design sitelen pona glyphs that can be easily recognized by the sitelen pona user community as it stands today. To that end, this document analyzes:

2 geometric fonts ( nasin-nanpa by jan Itan and linja laso by mute ante);
4 fonts closer to handwritten sitelen pona ( linja lipamanka by lipamanka, sitelen seli kiwen by Rebecca Bettencourt, nishiki-teki-lili by Umihotaru, and nasin sitelen pu by jan Ke Tami (recreates the glyphs from pu, still relevant to this day)).

This document will not document characters for obscure words or obscure alternate characters; only those discussed in the 3-tiered Common Sitelen Pona standard.

We start with basic sitelen pona anatomy and radicals. Characters with “heads” (mi, sina, ona, lape) are discussed first for their importance (mostly pronouns) and similarity. Then comes the word characters (ordered alphabetically), then the punctuation characters (ordered by usage tier).

If in doubt about glyph design, contact the association for advice!

Other than the fonts mentioned in this "About this site/document" section, this site uses TASA Orbiter by Local Remote.
